Math 141 Syllabus

Summer 2002

Course title: Calculus II
Lectures: MTuWThF 11:00 - 12:20 P.M.
Lectures location: Math 0103
Course website: Go to www.math.umd.edu/~szheng and click "Math 141"
Prerequisite: Math 140 or equivalent

Instructor: Shijun Zheng
Office: Room 4310, Math Building
Office time: 2:30-3:00 Monday through Friday, or by appointment
Phone: (301) 405 5094
E-mail:szheng@math.umd.edu

Required text: Ellis and Gulick, "Calculus with Analytic Geometry", 5th Edition

Syllabus. We will cover most of Chapters 6-10 in Ellis and Gulick. For the detailed syllabus, see the schedule hand-out. schedule of work

Grading. The weighting in your course score points (500 possible) will be
  • 200 Final Exam
  • 200 Two midterm exams
  • 100 Quizzes
(Point totals will be normalized to these relative weights. Section work will be possibly group work but including quizzes.)

Course Grades. Here is the correspondence of course grade and percentage of possible points scored:
A 90-100%, B 80-89%, C 70-79%, D 60-69%. F 0-59%.

Make-ups. Make-ups for quizzes will be given only for a very reasonable excuse.
**If you know BEFORE an exam that you have a conflict, contact me in advance. In this case, it is sometimes possible to arrange an early exam.**


Math 141 Tutorial Room --- Math 0403, 12:30-2:30 p.m.

Calculator. The use of calculator will be helpful for some homework and section quizzes. But it is not required to have knowledge of operating your calculator at an advanced level. A regular Texas Instruments graphics calculator (TI-82,83, ...) should be sufficient.
