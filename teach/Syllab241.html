Math 241 Syllabus

Spring 2003

Course title: Calculus III
Lectures: TuTh 2-2:50 (Section 0231) and 3-3:50 (Section 0241)
Lectures location: Math 0201
Course website: Go to www.math.umd.edu/~hck and click "Math 241"

Office: Room 4310, Math Building

Phone: (301) 405 5094

Required text: Ellis and Gulick, "Calculus with Analytic Geometry", 5th Edition

Syllabus. We will cover most of Chapters 11-15 in Ellis and Gulick. For the detailed syllabus, see the schedule. schedule of work

Grading. The weighting in your course score points (780 possible) will be
  • 200   Final Exam
  • 100 each   Four midterm exams (the lowerest one will be halved)
  • 50   Written homework
  • 50   Quizzes
  • 130   Eight Matlab projects
(Point totals will be normalized to these relative weights. Matlab assignment will be possibly group work.)

Course Grades. Your grade will be based on a point total, probably with a curve applied. There will be a total of 750 points: 200 for the final, 350 for the hour exams (100 points each but the lowest score is halved), and 100 for quizzes and homework, and 100 for computer projects. There will be four computer assignments which will be graded. You will be encouraged to work in small groups on these computer assignments. The preferred language will be Matlab. Previous Matlab experience is not required. A Matlab primer is available here. The mfile you need for multiple integration may be downloaded by clicking here.

Make-ups. Makeupsfor exams will be given only in extreme circumstances in accordance with official University policy. Except for unforseeable events, requests for a makeup must be made before the exam is given.
**If you know BEFORE an exam that you have a conflict, contact me in advance. In this case, it is sometimes possible to arrange an early exam.**

Calculator. The use of calculator could be helpful (but not necessary) for some homework and section quizzes. It is not required to have knowledge of operating your calculator at an advanced level. On the hour exams, however, graphing and programmable calculators will not be allowed. If you wish, you may use a cheap scientific calculator, but it should not be needed.
