Generalized Witch of Agnesi paradox: Let \(y=\frac{1}{(1+x)^a} \),  \(0.5 < a \leqslant 1\),   \(x\in [0,\infty)\).
Then the volume of the solid of rovolution about the curve's asymptote is given by \(V=\pi\int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(1+x)^{2a}}dx=\frac{\pi}{2a-1}\) but the surface area of the solid of revolution is \begin{align*} &S=2\pi\int_0^\infty y \sqrt{1+y'^2} dx=\infty! \end{align*}
